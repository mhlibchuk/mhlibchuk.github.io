    SEO in 2016 and Beyond

    It's crowded out there. More and more companies are using blogs, social media, video, infographics, and webinars to promote their brands, engage with customers, and boost their SEO rankings.

    How do we stand out in the crowd?

    Trends

    Relevance (determined by text) and authority (determined by links) remain the two most important ranking factors in SEO.
    Search engines don't pay much attention to <meta> keywords these days. Content quality metrics like social media shares, click-throughs, and dwell times have risen in importance.
    Search engines have placed increasing importance on page speed and mobile-friendly, responsive design. Pages that require horizontal scrolling or pinching-and-zooming, or take a long time to load are being demoted in rankings.
    Changes in how results pages are displayed provide new opportunities to stand out. Rich Snippets display ratings, images, and video thumbnails to visitors before they click a link.

    Rich Media and SEO

    Search engines still have trouble finding and indexing content in videos, images, and image maps. If you want rich media to be findable by engines, supplement it with crawlable text, including alt text, captions, subtitles, and even just plain old text. As a bonus, this provides a better experience for visitors on mobile devices and for those that rely on assistive technologies like screen readers.

    "To perform better in search engine listings, your most important content should be in HTML text format. Images, Flash files, Java applets, and other non-text content are often ignored or devalued by search engine crawlers, despite advances in crawling technology." https://moz.com/beginners-guide-to-seo/basics-of-search-engine-friendly-design-and-development

    If You Build It, They Still Might Not Come

    Marketing and promoting content and social media and corporate blogs are vital to SEO success these days. With so much content out there, it is essential that we become more social.

    [S]earch technology relies on the metrics of relevance and importance, and they measure those metrics by tracking what people do: what they discover, react, comment, and link to. So, you can’t just build a perfect website and write great content; you also have to get that content shared and talked about. https://moz.com/beginners-guide-to-seo/why-search-engine-marketing-is-necessary

    7 SEO Tips

    Be sure to keep these tips in mind when creating SEO-friendly content.

    • Write for your audience, and not for search engines. Tricking search engines by keyword stuffing or buying links no longer works.
    • Write engaging content that sets you apart from your competitors. Search engines love unique content.
    • Supplement rich media with crawlable text.
    • Spend time on blogs, forums, and social media. Learn what questions your audience has (and what language they're using).
    • Promote your content on the social media channels that your audience uses.
    • Put a premium on mobile-friendly content that loads quickly in the browser.
